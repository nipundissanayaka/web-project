Home Page: Introduction to Neuroscience and Brain Function

Neuroscience is the scientific study of the nervous system, including the brain, spinal cord, and peripheral nerves. It encompasses a wide range of disciplines, including biology, psychology, and medicine.

The human brain is an incredibly complex organ, consisting of billions of neurons that communicate through billions of synapses. It is responsible for our thoughts, emotions, memories, and behaviors.

Neuroplasticity: How the Brain Adapts and Changes

Neuroplasticity refers to the brain’s ability to adapt and reorganize itself in response to experiences, learning, and injury. This remarkable property allows the brain to form new neural connections throughout life.

It was once believed that brain development stops in childhood, but research shows that adults also retain significant plasticity, allowing them to continue learning and developing new skills.
